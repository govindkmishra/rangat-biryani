Our Story

The Soul of Rangat

"Rangat is not just a colour. It is the proof that the biryani was made right."

What Does Rangat Mean?

Rangat is a Punjabi word that doesn't translate cleanly. It means the colour — but not just any colour. It's the specific, warm, golden-saffron hue that rises from a biryani that was cooked with patience and real ingredients.

When you lift the dum seal and a cloud of fragrant steam rises — orange-gold, heavy with the smell of kesar, slow-cooked masala and aged basmati — that moment is rangat. That is what we're here to deliver to your door.

This kitchen exists because the best biryani Govind ever ate wasn't in a restaurant. It was made at home. In a regular kitchen. With a cast-iron deg, a slow fire, and no shortcuts.

The Philosophy

Not a Factory. A Kitchen.

Most food delivery businesses scale by cutting corners. A little less meat here, a substitute spice there, an artificial colour to cover what the real saffron should do.

Rangat was built on a different idea: deliver less, but deliver it right. That's why we only operate within 2 km. That's why we limit daily batches. That's why we're never open past 11 PM.

We'd rather you get one great biryani than us make a hundred mediocre ones.

The saffron we use is real kesar — the kind that turns the rice gold without any help. Every morning, it's soaked in warm milk. Every evening, it goes into the deg.

The basmati is aged. The chicken is marinated overnight. The dum is 45 minutes minimum — sealed with atta dough so nothing escapes.

This is your neighbourhood's biryani ritual. And it's FSSAI licensed, clean, and consistent.
